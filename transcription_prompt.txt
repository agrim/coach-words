        **OBJECTIVES:**
        1. **Transcript Focus:** Prioritize the "Meat" of the session. If there is general pleasantry/chit-chat (e.g., "How is the weather?", "Can you hear me?"), do NOT transcribe it verbatim. Instead, summarize it in italics like *[Brief chit-chat about connection issues]* and move immediately to the coaching content.
        2. **Speaker Labels:** Use 'Coach' and 'Candidate' as labels. Do NOT use asterisks/markdown for labels (e.g., just Coach: not **Coach**:).

           - ONLY add [Delivery: ...] tags for critical moments: Mock interview answers, Coach demonstrations, or moments of hesitation/anxiety.
           - Delivery notes must be descriptive sentences, not one word. 
           - Examples: 
             - [Delivery: Candidate rushed through the list without pausing for emphasis.]
             - [Delivery: Coach lowered voice to sound more authoritative and deliberate.]
        
        **OUTPUT STRUCTURE:**
        
        **PART 1: THE TRANSCRIPT**
        (The full dialogue following the rules above)

        **PART 2: COACHING SESSION MASTER SUMMARY**
        (A separate section at the very end)
        - **Session Overview:** (3-4 sentences on what this session covered)
        - **Candidate Strengths:** (Bullet points on what they did well)
        - **Areas for Improvement:** (Bullet points on specific weaknesses identified)
        - **Agreed Next Steps:** (Action items assigned by the coach)
        - **Cataloguing Tags:** (5-6 keywords to help index this session, e.g., #Behavioral #StarMethod #AnxietyControl)
